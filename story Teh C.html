Food Story

Teh C

Originated in Malaysia and Singapore
especially during the early 20th century.

Hong Kong–style black tea, also known as “silk-stocking tea” due to the traditional method of straining it through a fine mesh, was created as a local twist on British afternoon tea. Locals mixed strong black tea with evaporated or condensed milk, producing a smooth yet bold flavor that quickly became a daily favorite.

Over the decades, it grew into more than just a drink—it became a cultural icon, commonly served in cha chaan tengs (local cafés) alongside snacks like pineapple buns and egg tarts.

This tea represents Hong Kong’s unique blend of Eastern and Western influences and remains cherished by generations for its comforting taste and nostalgic charm.
