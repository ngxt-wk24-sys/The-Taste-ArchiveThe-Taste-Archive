Egg Tart

Originated in China
especially popular in Hong Kong during the mid-20th century.

Egg Tarts are small pastries filled with smooth, sweet egg custard, encased in either a flaky puff or shortcrust pastry. They became a staple in Hong Kong bakeries and tea houses, often enjoyed as a breakfast treat, snack, or dessert alongside milk tea. The delicate balance of creamy custard and crisp pastry made them an instant favorite, symbolizing the fusion of Western and Chinese baking traditions.

Over the years, bakers have experimented with flavors like chocolate, matcha, or cheese, yet the classic egg tart remains iconic—a nostalgic taste that evokes memories of Hong Kong’s bustling streets and cozy tea shops.
