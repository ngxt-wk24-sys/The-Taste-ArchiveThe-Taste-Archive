Food Story

Siu Mai

Originated in China
especially Guangdong Province.

Siu mai is a popular dim sum dumpling, usually filled with minced pork, shrimp, and mushrooms, wrapped in a thin wheat skin and left open at the top. Its bright yellow wrapper and round shape make it one of the most recognizable dishes in dim sum culture.

The origins of siu mai can be traced back to roadside teahouses and markets, where vendors sold small, affordable bites to travelers. Over time, it became a staple of Cantonese dim sum, enjoyed alongside tea in busy teahouses.

In traditional yum cha culture, siu mai is shared at the table, ordered in small bamboo baskets. Families and friends gather, chat, and slowly enjoy the meal, making the food part of a social experience rather than just something to eat.

Today, siu mai is served around the world, from traditional teahouses to modern restaurants. Despite variations in fillings and styles, it continues to represent sharing, comfort, and the lively spirit of dim sum culture.
