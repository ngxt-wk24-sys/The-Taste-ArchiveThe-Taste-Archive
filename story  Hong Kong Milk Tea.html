Hong Kong Milk Tea

Originated in Hong Kong
especially during the British colonial period.

Hong Kong milk tea, also known as “silk-stocking tea” because of the traditional method of straining it through a fine mesh, was created as a local adaptation of British afternoon tea. Locals blended strong black tea with evaporated or condensed milk, creating a smooth yet robust flavor that quickly became a daily staple.

Over the decades, it evolved into more than just a drink—it became a cultural icon, served in cha chaan tengs (local cafés) alongside traditional snacks like pineapple buns and egg tarts.

Hong Kong milk tea reflects the city’s unique fusion of Eastern and Western influences and continues to be cherished by generations for its comforting taste and nostalgic charm.
