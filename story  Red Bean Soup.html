Red Bean Soup

Originated in China
especially popular in southern regions such as Guangdong and Jiangsu.

Red Bean Soup is a classic Chinese dessert made from adzuki beans, sometimes sweetened with rock sugar and flavored with ingredients like dried tangerine peel or lotus seeds. Served hot or cold, it is cherished for its comforting sweetness and smooth texture. Traditionally enjoyed after meals or during festivals, red bean soup is not only a treat but also considered nourishing in Chinese culinary culture.

Over generations, families have passed down variations of the recipe, with some adding coconut milk, sago, or glutinous rice balls, keeping this humble dessert a beloved staple in Chinese homes and dessert shops alike.
