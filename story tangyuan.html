Food Story

Tangyuan

Originated in China
especially during the Song Dynasty.

Tangyuan, soft glutinous rice balls served in warm syrup or soup, is a traditional Chinese dessert closely associated with family reunion and harmony. Its round shape symbolizes completeness, unity, and togetherness, making it an essential dish during festivals, especially the Lantern Festival.

Legend has it that during the Song Dynasty, a clever young woman created small round rice balls filled with sweet sesame paste. When served in hot soup, the floating white balls resembled a bright full moon, representing hope and happiness. Over time, this dessert became known as Tangyuan, meaning “roundness in soup,” reflecting both its appearance and its cultural meaning.

In ancient times, families would gather to make tangyuan together, rolling the dough by hand and sharing stories while waiting for the pot to boil. Eating tangyuan was believed to bring good fortune and strengthen family bonds for the coming year.

Today, tangyuan comes in many variations, from classic black sesame and peanut fillings to modern flavors. Though styles may change, its meaning remains the same—a warm reminder of home, reunion, and shared moments.
