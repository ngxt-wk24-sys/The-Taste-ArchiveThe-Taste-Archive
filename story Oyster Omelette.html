Food Story

Oyster Omelette

Originated in Fujian and Taiwan
especially in coastal regions.

Oyster omelette is a popular street food made with fresh oysters, eggs, starch, and vegetables, pan-fried until slightly crispy on the outside and soft inside. Known for its rich texture and savory flavor, it reflects the close relationship between coastal communities and the sea.

The dish is believed to have originated in times when food was scarce. Fishermen and farmers combined locally available oysters with eggs and starch to create a filling and affordable meal. Over time, this simple dish became a staple at night markets and roadside stalls.

In coastal towns, oyster omelette is often cooked in open stalls, where the sizzling sound and aroma attract passersby. It is usually served hot, topped with a sweet-savory sauce, making it both comforting and lively.

Today, oyster omelette is strongly associated with night market culture and local identity. More than just a snack, it represents resourcefulness, community life, and the vibrant spirit of street food culture.
