Food Story

Pineapple Bun

Originated in Hong Kong
especially during the post-War era.

Despite its name, this sweet bun contains no pineapple; the name comes from its characteristic golden, crackly top that resembles a pineapple’s skin.

Pineapple buns quickly became a beloved staple in Hong Kong bakeries and tea houses. Traditionally enjoyed with a slab of butter inside—known as “bolo yau”—it combines a soft, fluffy interior with a sweet, crunchy top. Over the decades, it has not only become a symbol of Hong Kong’s culinary creativity but also a nostalgic treat for generations, often paired with milk tea for a classic breakfast or afternoon snack.

Even today, bakeries continue to innovate with variations such as custard-filled, chocolate, or even savory versions, keeping the spirit of the humble pineapple bun alive while appealing to modern tastes.
