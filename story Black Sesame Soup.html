Food Story

Black Sesame Soup

Originated in China
especially during the Han Dynasty.

Black Sesame Soup, a smooth and fragrant dessert, has long been cherished in Chinese cuisine. Traditionally enjoyed in cooler months, it is known for both its comforting taste and nourishing qualities.

Legend has it that a young scholar, studying long hours for the imperial exams, often skipped meals. His mother, worried about his health, ground black sesame seeds with rice flour to make a warm, sweet soup. The rich aroma and velvety texture not only gave him energy but also reminded him of her care.

Soon, black sesame soup became popular across villages. Families added rock sugar, glutinous rice balls, or lotus seeds, creating regional variations while keeping the heart of the dish: warmth and love.

Today, it remains a beloved dessert, evoking memories of home and family. Every spoonful carries the simple joys of life and the enduring bond between food and care.
