Food Story

Soy Milk

Originated in China
especially during the Han Dynasty.

Soy milk is one of the oldest plant-based drinks in East Asian food culture. Made by soaking, grinding, and boiling soybeans, it was developed as a nutritious and affordable alternative to dairy. For centuries, soy milk has been a daily staple in Chinese households, street markets, and breakfast stalls.

Often enjoyed hot and fresh in the morning, soy milk is commonly paired with youtiao (fried dough sticks) or steamed buns. Its mild flavor allows it to be served sweet or savory, reflecting the flexibility of traditional food culture.

Over time, it has become more than just a drink—soy milk represents a comforting start to the day, closely tied to memories of neighborhood breakfast stalls and everyday life across generations.
