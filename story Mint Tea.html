Food Story

Mint Tea

Originated in Middle
East
especially Morocco.

Mint tea has long been a symbol of hospitality and daily life in North Africa, especially in Morocco. Traditionally made by brewing green tea with fresh mint leaves and sugar, it is served throughout the day—to welcome guests, mark conversations, and offer comfort in both hot and cool weather.

Preparing mint tea is often a slow, mindful ritual. The tea is poured from a height to create foam, enhancing both aroma and taste. Beyond its refreshing flavor, mint tea is valued for its calming and digestive qualities, making it a drink associated with relaxation and warmth rather than urgency.

As trade and travel spread the culture of tea, mint tea moved beyond its place of origin and became enjoyed worldwide. Today, it represents more than a beverage—it reflects a way of life centered on sharing, patience, and quiet moments of connection.
