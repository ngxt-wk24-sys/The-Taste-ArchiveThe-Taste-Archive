Food Story

Turnip Cake

Originated in Southern China
especially Guangdong and Fujian regions.

Turnip cake, made from grated white radish mixed with rice flour and savory ingredients, is a traditional Chinese dish commonly enjoyed during Lunar New Year and family gatherings. Its soft texture and mild sweetness symbolize good fortune and steady progress.

Historically, white radish was inexpensive and widely available, especially in winter. Families used it to create a filling dish that could be shared among many people. The cake was steamed, then often pan-fried, making it both practical and comforting.

In many households, preparing turnip cake was a family activity. Elders would handle the seasoning, while younger members helped grate the radish or stir the batter. The process turned simple ingredients into a dish full of warmth and meaning.

Today, turnip cake is served not only at home but also in dim sum restaurants and street stalls. Though variations exist, it remains a symbol of tradition, togetherness, and the quiet joy of home-cooked food.
